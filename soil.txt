Soil Types and Crop Suitability Guide

There are different types of soil used for agriculture.

Alluvial Soil:
Found near river areas. Very fertile. Suitable for rice, wheat, sugarcane, and pulses.

Black Soil:
Also called regur soil. Good for cotton. Retains moisture well. Suitable for cotton, soybean, sunflower.

Red Soil:
Low in nutrients but responds to fertilizers. Suitable for millets, groundnut, pulses.

Laterite Soil:
Found in high rainfall areas. Suitable for tea, coffee, rubber, coconut.

Sandy Soil:
Drains water quickly. Suitable for groundnut, watermelon, coconut.

Clay Soil:
Holds water for long time. Suitable for paddy and rice crops.

Loamy Soil:
Best soil for agriculture. Balanced sand and clay. Suitable for vegetables, wheat, sugarcane.

Soil pH Guide:
pH below 6 = acidic soil
pH 6 to 7.5 = best for most crops
pH above 8 = alkaline soil

To improve soil fertility:
Add compost
Use green manure
Practice crop rotation
Add organic matter
Avoid overuse of chemicals
